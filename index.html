Wait - Windows 10 is Retiring?! What Do I Do Now?

Blog Post 1: GeekyChick's Guide To Staying Safe, Sane, and Speedy.

Windows 10 has reached it's end of life meaning no more free security updates. So with that said some users in due time may experience laggy performance and failed installations. You will also be vulnerable to malware and ransomware. If you have any questions regarding Windows 10 End of Support you can probably find your answer in the Q&A section just below. Heres a list of troubleshooting tips for windows 10 end-of-life

Step 1: Don't Panic, Your Laptop isn't going to Explode

  • Windows 10 officially stopped getting free updates on October 14th, 2025. That means no more free security patches, unless you upgrade or enroll into Extended Support. But you've got options.

Step 2: Check If You Can Upgrade to Windows 11

  • Start Menu System Settings Update & Security Windows Update Check for updates

    • If your Laptop says eligible for upgrade. You're golden. Hit that upgrade button and let the magic begin.

Step 3: If You Can't Upgrade, Get Extra Protection

  • Windows 10 is offering a extended security updates (ESU) for 1 more year. It's like a VIP Pass for your old laptop. You can opt in on Microsoft's Site or ask your friendly GeekChick ( that's me) to help.

Step 4: Clean-up Your Laptop Like It's Spring

Fix To-Do
Restart Seriously - it fixes more than you think
Clear junk Use Disk Cleanup or CCleaner
Malware Scan Run malewarebytes or windows Defender
Uinstall clutter Toss apps you haven't use since 2022
Free up space Aim for 20% free on your hard drive.
Upgrade Hardware RAM or SSD = speed boost

Step 5: Retire that old Tech

Throw out that old laptop

  • Backup your files to an external drive or to a cloud of your choose
  • Then toss that old laptop.
